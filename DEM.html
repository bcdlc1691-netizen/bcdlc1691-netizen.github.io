Assignment 3: DEM

Digital Elevation Models

In this assignment, we needed to use an old map of the Netherlands, along a river or canal, and make an elevation map using QGIS of the modern day state of the chosen area. Personally, I chose the area around Schiedam, west to Rotterdam. Rotterdam is an area which has undergone many changes in its waterways, and it was an interesting exercise also to see the cartographic perception and scale of hand-drawn maps.

Information about the old map

Drawn in 1749, by famous Dutch cartographer M. Bolstra, this is part of a series of old maps under Utrecht University's care. Below, see the map and the area today.



About the size and choices

The total area I worked with in this assignment was around 290 squared kilometres making it a medium-sized region of interest. As for display choices, the Amersfoort/RD New coordinate system for visibility and the colors were picked for intuitivity, with greens representing high elevation and blues representing low elevation, almost mimicking ocean and land.


Challenges in this assignment

This assignment was one of the most challenging ones, as I felt very lost with little guidance. However, I did manage to produce a result I am somewhat satisfied with. My main struggles this assignment were: creating layers for all 3 DTM, DSM, and RGB, map orientation, as the mapped area changed dramatically, and lastly, rendering hillshade in QGIS was not running correctly for me. Most of these issues were trivial so I still was able to present a product.
