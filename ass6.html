Assignment 6: Multicriteria Analysis

What is MCA?

MCA is a structured approach used to analyze possible alternatives and preferences and evaluate them under different criteria at the same time. In MCA, goals are specified and corresponding characteristics and indicators are recognized. Real-world spatial decisions do not depend on a single factor. Infrastructure planning, EIAs, and resource allocation all need multi-variable consideration. Read more: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/chapter/referencework/pii/B9780124095489096986 and https://spatial-eye.com/blog/spatial-analysis/what-is-multi-criteria-analysis-in-gis/

My ideal project

In this project I would like to tackle the issue of informal housing in the outskirts (conos) of Metropolitan central Lima and lack of accessibility to wheelchair users/limited mobility.

For example, the map below already shows how the conos have incredibly limited access to water services. I would also need to explain the history and socioeconomic context of the formation of conos and informal housing (what I’d be focusing on). It would be a very intersectional paper talking about classism, racism, economic and social mobility, environmental health and care, plus climatic factors that make Lima brown and grey (Humboldt current, north of Atacama), excluding lomas which are already protected and exploited in its natural recreational value.


Some criteria to have into consideration would be the elevation, land cover, population density, demographics, and overall development of the area. Additionally, I would need to decide if I am deeply analyzing one district or comparing multiple and seeing differences in accessibility.

Working it out - GIS

The basemap layer could be either topographic or political. It would be interesting to do both as well, to see if the roads are mapped in the first place. If not, georeferencing and data collection would be needed. So, a layer with the polygons of the buildings and mapped roads. Also, using a layer for elevation could be good. I think that this project would need multiple maps. Maybe also points with accessibility data collected from a computed variable based on roads, opinion from residents, etc.
